Forex trading involves substantial risk of loss. The following risks should be carefully considered:

  • Leverage Risk: Forex trading typically involves high leverage, which can amplify both profits and losses. A small market movement can result in proportionally larger losses (or gains) relative to your margin.
  • Market Volatility: Currency markets can be highly volatile. Prices can move rapidly and unpredictably due to economic events, political developments, or market sentiment.
  • Liquidity Risk: In certain market conditions, it may be difficult to execute trades at desired prices, particularly during news events or market gaps.
  • Gap Risk: Markets can "gap" — open at a significantly different price than the previous close — potentially causing losses beyond your stop loss.
  • Counterparty Risk: You bear the risk that your broker may fail to meet its financial obligations to you.
  • Currency Risk: If you trade in currencies other than your base currency, exchange rate fluctuations can affect your returns.

While not financial advice, we strongly encourage responsible trading practices:

  • Use Stop Losses: Always use stop loss orders to limit potential losses on every trade.
  • Manage Position Sizes: Risk only a small percentage of your account per trade (commonly 1-2%).
  • Don't Overtrade: Taking every signal is not required. Be selective.
  • Use Demo Accounts: Practice with demo accounts before risking real money.
